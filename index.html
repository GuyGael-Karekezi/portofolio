About Me

My name is Ishimwe Karekezi Guy Gael. I laid my professional foundation at the University of Rwanda, where I studied Electronics and Telecommunications Engineering. During this time, I focused on bridging the gap between theoretical concepts and real-world applications. One of my cornerstone projects was developing a Smart Energy Meter, an IoT-based solution designed to empower households by allowing them to track their electricity consumption in real time.

Currently, I am advancing my expertise at Carnegie Mellon University Africa, pursuing a Master of Science in Engineering Artificial Intelligence. My academic and research efforts are centered on machine learning, data analysis, and the development of intelligent systems that leverage data to solve complex problems. I am a strong advocate for "clean code" and believe that the most effective solutions are often built on simple, elegant ideas and models that deliver transparent, high-impact results.

Driven by a passion for innovation, my ultimate goal is to build practical, intelligent tools that simplify lives. I am deeply interested in the intersection of data-driven systems, machine learning, and IoT. I thrive in collaborative environments, valuing teamwork as a vital catalyst for personal growth and the creation of superior technical solutions. I am always eager to explore new ideas and discover more sophisticated ways to interpret and utilize data for the greater good.
